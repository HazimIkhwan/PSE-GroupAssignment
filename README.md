# UniHealth - Track | Earn | Stay Healthy

Our project is the UniHealth system, a system designed to help assist university students in keeeping track of their health. This aligns with our chosen SDG, which is Good Health and Well-Being (SDG 3), as we recognize that university students can sometimes be so busy they don't have time to take care of their health.

This project offers a variety of ways to take care of a student's health, such as tracking the amount of steps they have taken, their daily water intake and their BMI. Futhermore, the above processes has been put through gamification to make it easier and more fun for students to do.

And lastly, it also has a built in feature to make appointments with near clinics to ease the process of making a medical check up.

# Project Description

For a more detailed description of the project, as stated before this project aims to allow university students to manage their health in an easier and more accessible way. The reason why university students were chosen is because they are constantly busy managing multiple things at once which is why we deem it could be helpful to introduce them to a health tracker app, this is so that instead of thinking about multiple topics at once such as how many steps I took, how many water did I drink today, what was my calorie intake, they can just open the app instead which makes the process of tracking their health easier by making it all accessible from just one app.

Moving onto its features, while it does have regular health tracker features, our main highlight that can set it apart from other similar apps is the fact that we have gamified some of the tracker process, we chose this decision because when processes are gamified they become more enjoyable to do. An example of this is that in our app the Health of the student is tracked using a "Health Bar" instead of just numbers, and it can incetivize students to fill up that healthbar by doing more healthy activities.

And lastly onto one of our more important features, we have included a way to book and cancel appointments using our app. The reason why this was chosen because similar to the first reasoning students are very busy which is why we decided to introduce an easier way for students to meet doctors face to face without having to physically walk to the cilinic. Additionally, we also added a way for students to cancel said appointments if they have changed their mind.


# Overview of Your SDLC Work

| SDLC Phase | What we did |
| --- | --- |
| Requirements gathering | The first phase where we discussed what a student would want in a health tracker app |
| Systems analysis | After that, futher research was done to extract useful infromation from the previous discussion, such as determining functional and non functional requirements. Thus, we came to the conclusion that students would be more likely to keep track of their health if the process of taking care of your health was more like a video game.   |
| Design | After understanding the requirements, we started to design the how the health tracker app would look, we used Figma to design how the app would look, and we tried to design a UI that would be intuitive and easy to use |
| Implementation | This process also ended up using Figma as we managed to create a rudimentary interactive prototype that showed how the app would function and look like. |
| Testing | Testing was done by giving some of our friends the prototype and we documented their feedback on what they felt after seeing and using it, and what are the improvements if they had any. |
| Deployment and Maintenance | After receiving the prototype feedback, we were quick to go through them to see which ones could be used and which were unhelpful, this allowed us to improve the software in a more meaningful way. |
